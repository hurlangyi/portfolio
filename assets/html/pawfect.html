the challenge

How can aspiring dog owners adopt new companions during a pandemic?

The pet adoption process was already fraught with surprising adoption requirements and chaotic meet-and-greets even before the pandemic. Now, with limited shelter appointments and restrictive social distancing measures, potential owners are finding adoption even more difficult than ever.

My teammates and I were tasked to focus on one part of the pet adoption process and create a mobile app and desktop site that address the problems and opportunities within this focus area. Based on our secondary and user research, we decided to analyze the process of scheduling meetups that allow potential owners to meet with shelter dogs in person.

the process

user research

understanding pet adoptions

Although several of us have adopted dogs before, none of us had tried adopting a pet during a pandemic. Our team first conducted some general research on the uplifting increase of adoptions during quarantine and how shelters and adoption processes have adapted to the new circumstances.

Customers

People are not realizing the long-term commitments pets entail after adoption, especially when this pandemic ends.

Shelters

Many shelters are navigating the sudden surge of adoptions and pandemic barriers through methods like virtual meet-and-greets, scheduled in-person appointments, and curbside adoptions.

interviewing pet adopters

To verify or refute our research, our team interviewed 12 different pet owners. We reached out to a mixture of people who had adopted prior to and during the pandemic to see if there were any significant similarities or differences between the circumstances. We asked them about how they came to adopt their pet(s) and the frustrations and highlights from their individual adoption processes.

“There were a lot more steps than I had anticipated.”
“The websites feel like a bait and switch because the dogs are adopted so quickly.”
“I want to meet and interact with the dog before I decide to adopt them.”

For each interview, we also made a brief journey map to understand the goals, actions, and experiences throughout each person’s pet adoption.

user insights

Many of our participants initially planned to adopt their pets from shelters, but ultimately chose different methods to obtain their pets due to the following insights:

People are surprised by the elaborate requirements for adoption.

Several users were surprised by some shelters’ extremely strict adoption requirements, like home visits and letters of recommendation. Potential owners are often unaware of these requirements before starting the process and become deterred from shelter adoptions.

Many shelters don’t update their website information in real-time.

Many shelter websites don’t immediately update which listed dogs have been adopted. This is frustrating for users who grow attached to certain dogs on the websites, only to learn that they’ve already been adopted.

Meeting the dogs in person is stressful due to foster parents and/or competing potential owners.

Interacting with a dog is uncomfortable when the foster parent is hovering behind and pressuring the user to adopt the dog. Pet adoption events are stressful due to the chaos of many people trying to adopt a limited number of pets. Neither of these in-person interactions are viable during the pandemic.

customer journey mapping

Based on these insights, we created our persona Alice, a recent college graduate looking to adopt a shelter dog during the pandemic. We then synthesized our general research and user interviews into a customer journey map of Alice’s adoption process. I was responsible for designing and organizing our journey maps.

Our initial customer journey map

When presenting our journey map, we were recommended to narrow our scope and focus on a specific part of the adoption process. Based on our user research, we decided it would be best to focus on the experience of scheduling in-person meetings with the dogs and updated our journey map to reflect this.

Our revised customer journey map

finalizing our solution's goals

After analyzing our user research and feedback, we ultimately decided that our solution needed to meet the following 2 goals:

Help potential owners meet dogs in person quickly and easily.

Make information about the dogs more transparent and personal.

design

To help our team visualize what screens we needed to make and how our users would navigate the website, we created user flow and information architecture maps of our website’s pages. Through this process, we decided to narrow our solution’s scope to focus on scheduling in-person meetings with shelter dogs.

“paper” prototyping

To verify our established user flow and website’s usability, we created a series of lo-fi “paper” prototype based on our proposed screens and conducted a series of think-aloud tests. We focused on a personalized “swipe” homepage to let users discover and schedule times with different dogs. We asked our participants to imagine themselves as potential dog owners wanting to schedule a meeting with one of their favorited dogs on the website.

mid-fi prototyping

We created our mobile mid-fi wireframes based on our paper mockups and their respective feedback. Our teammate Lauren created a loose style guide to implement across our screens, which I then translated into our first desktop designs. We had several of our classmates user test our mid-fi prototype in order to understand whether our platform’s concept and design language were clear and used their insights to guide the rest of our design experience.

finalizing our style guide

To establish trust and a sense of reliability within our service, we opted for a single font and an emphasis on navy blue with accent colors against a white backdrop. We wanted to make sure our service’s UI was consistent across both the app and desktop site.

hi-fi prototyping

Utilizing all of the feedback we had received, we finalized our high-fidelity wireframes. We made sure to reorganize our visual hierarchy and emphasize calls to actions so that our users can scan relevant information more quickly and easily navigate the platforms’ different features.

the final solution

Pawfect Match is a digital dog adoption platform that lets users see personalized dog recommendations and schedule safe, in-person meetups with potential pets.

View and filter recommended dogs

Get personalized shelter dog recommendations that best match you based on your onboarding application and refine your results through the search filter. All of the dogs listed on Pawfect Match have their information updated regularly and are available for adoption!

Individually view and add dogs to your favorites

Go through your recommended results through a card view to individually assess each dog. Add any dogs that catch your eye to your Favorites.

Schedule safe in-person meetups with the dogs

Read through a dog’s profile to learn about their backstory, health history, and your qualifications to adopt them. When scheduling to meet with them in person, you can choose the location, who will bring the dog to the meetup, the date, and time of the day.

Review your upcoming meetups

View your upcoming meetups to review the meeting’s details and COVID-19 protocols. You can also reschedule the time, change the location, or contact the shelter worker or foster parent bringing the dog over.

Reflections & Next Steps

Focus on one aspect of your user's journey.

Although my teammates and I dove into this project wanting to address the myriad of pain points we discovered in the adoption process, we realized that this would be very difficult for us and overwhelm our users from an overload of information. Focusing on helping adopters discover and safely meet dogs in person helped ground our approach and design process.

Always consider how to implement your designs on different platforms.

This project challenged me to design the same service for multiple platforms and ensure that the visual language and information hierarchy remained consistent throughout the different viewports. Consistency and readability help establish trust in our users.
